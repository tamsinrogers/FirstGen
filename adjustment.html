We asked our parents to describe some of the biggest social and cultural differences they noticed in the United States.
Here's what they said:

→ The most essential thing is to form friendships and connections with other
immigrants in the United States. Even if they are not from your home country,
you are going through the same experience together, and it is
important to have friends who share this with you.

→ Differences in greetings and sayings

→ As a child immigrant, it is typical for one to feel as though they do not
have control over what is happening. This is because minors are not
the ones applying for citizenship, but they are still subject
to the hardships that many other foreigners face.

→ It is still common for new citizens to feel and be treated like outsiders,
regardless of the fact that they now hold a US citizenship.

→ The journey and challenges you have overcome are incredible,
even if you do not always feel valued or welcome, you are not alone.
Immigrants will always make this country great.
